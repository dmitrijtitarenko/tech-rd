# RegTech & Compliance Research Domain

## Overview

RegTech and compliance research synthesizes regulatory requirements, audit frameworks, and technology solutions to enable organizations to manage compliance obligations cost-effectively. This domain spans financial regulation (PCI-DSS, AML), data protection (GDPR, CCPA), information security (ISO27001, SOC2), and health data privacy (HIPAA).

Effective research here means understanding: which regulations actually apply to your business? What's the minimum viable compliance effort? How do you build evidence trails to pass audits efficiently?

## Key Research Dimensions

**Regulatory Frameworks**
- **GDPR**: EU data protection, global implications for EU residents' data
- **CCPA/CPRA**: California and state-level privacy laws
- **HIPAA**: Healthcare data protection, covered entity requirements
- **PCI-DSS**: Payment card industry data security standards
- **GLBA**: Financial privacy (banks, insurance)
- **SOX**: Financial reporting controls (public companies)

**Compliance Standards & Certifications**
- **SOC2 Type I**: Trust services criteria audit (point-in-time)
- **SOC2 Type II**: Controls over time (usually 6-12 months)
- **ISO27001**: Information security management system certification
- **ISO27002**: Code of practice (non-certified guidance)
- **ISO9001**: Quality management (less tech-focused)
- **FedRAMP**: Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program (US government)

**Industry-Specific Requirements**
- **Finance**: KYC (Know Your Customer), AML (Anti-Money Laundering), transaction reporting
- **Healthcare**: HIPAA BAA (Business Associate Agreements), de-identification standards
- **Data Processing**: DPA (Data Processing Agreements), subprocessor disclosure
- **Manufacturing**: Environmental, safety, product liability compliance
- **Multi-industry**: NIST Cybersecurity Framework, CIS Controls

**Audit & Assessment Approaches**
- **Internal audits**: Self-assessment questionnaires, continuous monitoring
- **External audits**: Third-party verification, certification bodies
- **Continuous compliance**: Real-time monitoring vs point-in-time audits
- **Audit evidence**: Documentation, logs, access controls, training records
- **Remediation**: How to address findings and maintain compliance

**Technology & Automation**
- **GRC platforms**: ServiceNow, Workiva, Domo for compliance management
- **Identity & access management**: Okta, Azure AD for access control evidence
- **Logging & monitoring**: Splunk, CloudTrail for audit trails
- **Contract management**: Ironclad, Doctrine for regulatory obligations tracking
- **Automation**: How to reduce manual compliance work

## Data Sources & Query Methods

**Regulatory Guidance & Official Sources**
- Regulatory agency websites: ICO (GDPR), FTC (CCPA), OCR (HIPAA)
- Guidance documents: GDPR Article 29 Working Party, NIST publications
- Enforcement actions: Real fines and requirements from agencies
- Industry associations: Standards for your sector (healthcare, finance, etc.)
- Query pattern: "GDPR requirement", "HIPAA compliance", "PCI-DSS checklist"

**Compliance Framework Documentation**
- Official framework documents (SOC2 Trust Service Criteria, ISO27001)
- Audit guides: How to evidence compliance
- Implementation templates: Sample policies, procedures
- Compliance mapping: Which regulation requires what
- Query pattern: "SOC2 Type II requirements", "ISO27001 implementation"

**Technology & GRC Tool Assessment**
- Gartner Magic Quadrant for GRC platforms
- G2 reviews for specific tool evaluation
- Case studies: How organizations implemented compliance
- Integration analysis: Does tool connect to your existing systems?
- Query pattern: "GRC platform comparison", "compliance automation"

**Vendor Security Assessments**
- Vendor questionnaires: What questions to ask suppliers
- Third-party audit results: Does vendor have SOC2/ISO27001?
- Security questionnaire databases: Standardized tools like Vendr
- Subprocessor disclosures: Who has access to data?
- Query pattern: "vendor due diligence", "subprocessor list"

**Cost & Timeline Analysis**
- Audit firm pricing (varies by scope and control testing hours)
- Implementation timeline: How long to get certified?
- Ongoing maintenance: Quarterly, annual requirements
- Remediation costs: Fixing control failures
- Query pattern: "SOC2 audit cost", "GDPR implementation timeline"

## Analysis Frameworks

**Regulatory Applicability Matrix**
Build a table: Regulations x Your Business Model
- Which regulations apply? (Geography, industry, data type)
- What's the enforcement risk? (Fine amounts, litigation likelihood)
- Implementation complexity: (Simple policy changes vs major architecture)
- Competitive requirement: (Do competitors have it?)
- Customer requirement: (Do enterprise customers require it?)

**Compliance Implementation Roadmap**
- **Phase 1**: Quick wins (policy documentation, training)
- **Phase 2**: Access control implementation (SSO, role-based access)
- **Phase 3**: Logging and monitoring (audit trails, alerting)
- **Phase 4**: Formal assessment (external audit, remediation)
- **Phase 5**: Continuous monitoring (real-time compliance tracking)

**SOC2 Audit Strategy**
- **Scope**: Which systems/services in audit scope?
- **Controls**: How many controls do you operate? (50-200 typical)
- **Testing period**: Type I (snapshot) or Type II (6-12 months)?
- **Auditor selection**: Big 4 accounting firms vs specialized firms
- **Evidence gathering**: What documentation proves control operation?
- **Remediation**: Timeline to fix findings before certification

**GDPR Compliance Checklist** (by principle)
- **Lawfulness**: Do you have legal basis to process data?
- **Purpose Limitation**: Are you using data only for stated purposes?
- **Data Minimization**: Collecting only necessary data?
- **Accuracy**: Processes to keep data current?
- **Storage Limitation**: Retention and deletion procedures?
- **Integrity & Confidentiality**: Security measures in place?
- **Accountability**: Documentation of processing decisions?

**Cost-Benefit Analysis for Compliance**
- **Compliance cost**: Implementation + audit + ongoing maintenance
- **Risk reduction**: What incidents does compliance prevent?
- **Revenue enablement**: Can you serve customers requiring certification?
- **Insurance discount**: Do insurers reduce premiums for compliance?
- **Operational efficiency**: Does compliance improve processes?

## Key Metrics to Always Collect

For compliance decisions, track:
1. **Regulatory coverage**: % of applicable regulations with documented compliance
2. **Control effectiveness**: # of controls operating effectively / total controls
3. **Audit results**: Open findings, severity, remediation status
4. **Evidence quality**: % of controls with sufficient documentation
5. **Process efficiency**: Audit preparation time, evidence gathering time
6. **Cost**: Audit fees, tool costs, team costs, remediation costs
7. **Risk**: Material findings, enforcement actions, audit failures

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Compliance theater**: Documentation that doesn't reflect reality
- **Audit surprise**: Discovering gaps during external audit (conduct self-assessment first)
- **Cost underestimation**: Compliance takes more time and money than expected
- **Scope creep**: Adding regulations without prioritizing
- **Tool sprawl**: Too many disconnected tools create maintenance burden
- **Personnel turnover**: Compliance knowledge walks out door if not documented
- **Regulatory changes**: GDPR enforcement evolves; compliance is continuous, not one-time
- **Subprocessor neglect**: Your compliance only as good as your vendors' compliance
