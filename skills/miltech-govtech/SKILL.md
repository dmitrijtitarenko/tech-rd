# Military Tech & Government Tech Research Domain

## Overview

Military tech and government tech research synthesizes regulatory frameworks, procurement processes, and technical standards that govern defense and federal technology sales. This domain encompasses export controls (ITAR/EAR), federal compliance (FedRAMP), government contracting (SAM.gov), and innovation programs (SBIR/STTR).

Effective research here means understanding: what does ITAR compliance actually require? How does the FedRAMP authorization process work? What are realistic timelines for government procurement?

## Key Research Dimensions

**Defense Technology & Dual-Use**
- Classified information handling: Secret, Top Secret, compartmented
- Controlled technologies: Cryptography, satellites, avionics, autonomous systems
- Dual-use items: Technologies with both commercial and military applications
- Technology transfer: Restrictions on sharing with foreign nationals
- Cybersecurity requirements: Unique for defense (CMMC, NIST frameworks)
- Operational security (OPSEC): Protecting classified information

**Export Controls & Compliance**
- ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations): Department of State controls
- EAR (Export Administration Regulations): Department of Commerce controls
- OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control): Sanctions enforcement
- De minimis rule: Threshold for triggering export controls
- Technical data restrictions: Which information requires export licenses?
- Compliance infrastructure: How to build compliant processes

**Federal Certification & Standards**
- FedRAMP: Authorizes cloud services for federal use (P-ATO = authorization)
- Moderate vs High: Different security controls based on sensitivity
- Continuous monitoring: Maintaining authorization over time
- FISMA: Federal Information Security Management Act requirements
- NIST SP 800-53: Security and privacy controls baseline
- Assessment and Authorization (A&A): The certification process itself

**Government Procurement**
- SAM.gov: System for Award Management (federal contractor registration)
- CAGE code: Commercial and Government Entity identification
- NAICS code: Industry classification for contract matching
- Contracting vehicles: Set-Asides, GSA Schedule, BPA agreements
- Small business requirements: 8(a), HUBZone, Woman-owned, Veteran-owned
- Bid & proposal process: How to respond to government RFQs/RFPs

**Innovation Programs**
- SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research): Phase I (proof of concept), Phase II (development)
- STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer): SBIR variant requiring university partnership
- DoD/Navy/Air Force specific programs: Technology agnostic funding
- Topic selection: How to identify relevant research areas
- Funding amounts: Typical Phase I ($150k-250k), Phase II ($750k-1.5M)
- Success rates: Very competitive (10-20% typical)

**Cybersecurity & Compliance Frameworks**
- CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification): DoD contractor requirement
- DIB (Defense Industrial Base): Designation affecting compliance requirements
- Zero Trust principles: Now required for federal systems
- Incident reporting: Requirements and timelines
- Personnel security: Clearance requirements and vetting

## Data Sources & Query Methods

**Export Control & Compliance Information**
- State Department ITAR guidance and technical advisory data
- Commerce Department EAR regulations and commodity jurisdiction
- OFAC sanctions lists and embargoed countries
- Industry associations: AIA (aerospace), NDS (defense)
- Compliance consulting firms: Expert guidance on interpretation
- Query pattern: "ITAR requirements", "export control [technology]"

**FedRAMP & Federal Compliance**
- FedRAMP.gov: Official authorization information, authorized providers list
- GSA documentation: FedRAMP process and controls
- NIST publications: SP 800-53, SP 800-171 guidance
- Assessment organizations: List of authorized 3PAOs
- Query pattern: "FedRAMP authorization timeline", "FedRAMP cost"

- SAM.gov: Federal contract data, opportunity announcements
- USASPENDING.gov: Government spending data and trends
- Contract databases: FedBizOpps, contractor performance history
- Small business resources: SBA (Small Business Administration)
- Query pattern: "government contract opportunity", "federal procurement"

**SBIR/STTR Programs**
- SBIR.gov: Official solicitation information and agency topics
- SBIR databases: Historical awards and success analysis
- Agency-specific programs: DoD, NSF, NIH, NASA SBIR offices
- Success stories: Which companies won and progressed to Phase II/III
- Query pattern: "SBIR topic [area]", "SBIR funding opportunity"

**Defense Market & Intelligence**
- DefenseNews, MilitaryTimes: Industry trends and contracts
- Government Accountability Office (GAO): Contract disputes and oversight
- Contractor databases: Who's winning contracts in your space?

- Query pattern: "defense market trends", "military technology demand"

## Analysis Frameworks

**ITAR Applicability Matrix**
- Is your technology on the USML (United States Munitions List)?
- Does your software control export-controlled technologies?
- Do you employ or interact with foreign nationals?
- Will products be exported or used outside the US?
- Are you sharing technical data (including documentation)?
- Do you need an ITAR authorization (license, exemption, or no control)?

**FedRAMP Timeline & Cost Estimate**
- **Preparation**: 3-6 months (security controls implementation)
- **Assessment**: 2-3 months (3PAO testing and documentation)
- **Authorization**: 1-2 months (agency decision)
- **Total**: 6-11 months typical (can be longer)
- **Cost**: $150k-$500k+ depending on system complexity
- **Ongoing**: Annual continuous monitoring requirements

**Government Contracting Readiness Checklist**
- DUNS number: Required for federal procurement
- SAM.gov registration: Must be registered and active
- CAGE code: Commercial identification for contracting
- Accounting systems: Compliant cost accounting standards
- Facility security: Compliant with contractor requirements
- Compliance infrastructure: Policies, training, auditing processes

**SBIR Opportunity Evaluation**
- Topic match: Does your technology fit available research areas?
- Feasibility: Can you accomplish Phase I objectives?
- Market: Is there commercial viability after government funding?
- Competition: How many proposals typically received? (affects odds)
- Team: Do you have right technical expertise?
- Timeline: Can you meet Phase I 12-month timeline?

**Export Control Technology Assessment**
- Is technology controlled? (Check CCL - Commerce Control List, USML)
- De minimis threshold: Is use of foreign content below 25%?
- Encryption strength: Does it trigger encryption controls (EAR Part 740)?
- Source data: Are you using publicly available information only?
- Patent status: Are there patent-pending technologies involved?
- Remediation: What changes would remove controls?

**Market Entry Strategy for Government Sector**
- **Phase 1**: SBIR/STTR for R&D funding and market validation
- **Phase 2**: Scale technology based on Phase I results
- **Phase 3**: Commercialization and government sales
- **FedRAMP**: Pursue if cloud/SaaS service (required for federal sales)
- **Contracting**: Leverage GSA Schedule or set-asides for small business
- **Partnership**: Teaming with established contractors accelerates entry

## Key Metrics to Always Collect

For government tech decisions, track:
1. **Compliance status**: ITAR authorization, FedRAMP status, CMMC certification
2. **Export control**: Technology classification, export license requirements
3. **Procurement readiness**: SAM.gov registration, CAGE code, system certifications
4. **Market opportunity**: Government spending in your sector, contract trends
5. **Timeline**: How long to compliance vs market window
6. **Cost**: Compliance investment, certification costs, ongoing maintenance
7. **Risk**: Export violation penalties, contract suspension, debarment

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **ITAR blindness**: Assuming your technology isn't controlled (many software systems are)
- **Foreign national surprise**: Employing non-US citizens with security clearance access is restricted
- **FedRAMP timeline underestimation**: 6-11 months is realistic; don't plan for faster
- **Compliance cost underestimation**: FedRAMP and certifications cost $200k-$500k+
- **Export license delays**: Getting ITAR license can take 6-12 months; plan accordingly
- **SBIR odds misunderstanding**: 10-20% selection rate means persistence required
- **Facility security neglect**: Physical security and access controls required for defense work
- **Continuous monitoring surprise**: FedRAMP requires ongoing annual assessment, not one-time fix
